Graduate Work

Neuromodulation of Feedback

My major interests that I am pursuing in my graduate work revolve around how sensory processing is impacted by dynamically changing internal states. In the Disney Lab, I study the localization of muscarinic acetylcholine receptors in primary visual cortex (V1) and their impact on signals from higher visual areas in the macaque.

Electron Microscopy

To establish the modulatory mechanisms of feedback in visual cortex, I am characterizing the ultrastructure of synapses made by feedback projecting neurons in primate visual cortex.

Learn more

In Vivo Electrophysiology

I am building experiments to test the function of muscarinic receptors on feedback via pharmacological peturbation of cortical microcircuits.

Previous Training

Taste Coding in Rodent Brain Stem

As a technician, I worked in the lab of Patricia DiLorenzo. My first experience with animal models and the coding of sensory information. There I worked on projects relating to changes in the sensory circuit resultant from diet-induced obesity in the rat.

Human Vision & Electrophysiology

As an undergraduate researcher with Dr. Vladimir Miskovic at Binghamton University, I gained my first experience with electrophysiology and time series data. My honors thesis demonstrated differential entrainment of EEG responses utilizing natural stimuli and artificial visuospatial noise. The lab had a number of other projects applying stress and aversive learning to visual perception and I'm still pursuing similar questions in my graduate work.
