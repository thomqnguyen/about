About

I'm a PhD Student at Duke University, where I study how acetylcholine and other neuromodulators impact integration of sensory information. I am passionate about where science interacts with the public and where we can do good. Outside of the lab, I'm engaged in community building among Duke PhD students, birdwatching, and appreciating parts of the world that are often overlooked.

Learn more

Neuroanatomy & Electrophysiology

I study the anatomical underpinnings of how sensory signals are modulated by acetylcholine in primates. As a graduate student in the Disney Lab at Duke, I use electron microscopy and in vivo electrophysiology to understand how cholinergic receptor localization impacts extrastriate feedback to primary visual cortex in the primate.

Intersections of Science

Academic science is at the intersection of research institutions, legislative bodies, and the individuals conducting research. We cannot divorce science from the society that supports it. I am passionate about issues where science and the public meet: birds & wildlife conservation, unionism's role in changing academia, the philosophy & utility of basic science.
